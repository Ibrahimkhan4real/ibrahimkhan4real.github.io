Reinforcement Learning & Control Researcher

I am Muhammad Ibrahim Khan, a PhD researcher in reinforcement learning at Coventry University. My work focuses on Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) and RL for energy-efficient control of heat pumps and domestic hot-water immersion systems, with a background in applied AI for real-world systems.

About

I work at the intersection of reinforcement learning, optimal control, and energy systems. My research looks at how planning methods such as MCTS and model-based RL can control heating systems under uncertainty, while respecting comfort constraints and minimising energy use. I am particularly interested in deadline-aware control of hot-water tanks and heat pumps, where the goal is to hit a temperature target at a specified time using as little energy as possible.

Selected projects

Deadline-aware control of domestic hot-water tanks

A Gymnasium environment and set of baselines for controlling an immersion hot-water heater with first-order thermal losses and discrete on/off actions. The agent must reach a temperature target at a fixed deadline while minimising energy use. I compare bang-bang control, zero-shot MCTS, and PPO policies under identical physics.

Hybrid MCTS for heat-pump control

Ongoing work on combining model-based planning and learned value functions to control residential heat pumps. The aim is to exploit MCTS for look-ahead planning while using RL to generalise across different building and weather conditions.

LLM-based code grader

An automated code-grading pipeline using open-source LLMs (for example, Llama 3) to evaluate candidate submissions. The system combines logical reasoning about code with black-box tests to provide fast, scalable technical screening.
